Improvement Recommendations

  1. Clarity: Add more context to the story to make it easier to understand. For example, what type of prescriptions are we talking about (e.g., medication, lab results, etc.)?
  2. Completeness: Specify acceptance criteria for the story, such as:
    • What are the valid and invalid states for the prescription approval workflow?
    • How will errors be handled (e.g., duplicate prescriptions, invalid dosages)?
  3. Business Value Alignment: While the story has a strong focus on compliance, consider adding more details on how this will improve patient safety or operational efficiency.
  4. Testability: Provide more information on the system actors involved in the approval workflow (e.g., doctor, pharmacist, patient). This will help QA engineers derive more comprehensive test cases.
  5. Technical Feasibility: While the story is technically feasible, consider adding more details on the integration with existing EHR systems and any potential technical challenges.

Example of Improved User Story

As a doctor, I want to review and approve patient prescriptions digitally before they reach the pharmacy, ensuring safety and compliance, by integrating with our existing EHR system and reducing the risk of medication errors.
